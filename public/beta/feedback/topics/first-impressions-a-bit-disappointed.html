First of all, I'd like to say thank you for inviting me to the beta even if I haven't purchased GWG yet. I was having issues that put me on the fence, and I was hoping GWG2 would fix all those issues for good. Unfortunately, it doesn't seem to be the case.

https://gameplay.gestureworks.com/gameplay_forums/forums/feedback/topics/a-review-of-gwg-and-suggestions-i-have--2

I wrote this for GWG1 a few months ago, and even though I never got replies, a few of the things I mentioned got fixed, but there are things that are still present even in GWG2.

  • The new controller editor is better. Things like the grid and numbers for scale and opacity mean everything can be a lot more precise, and I like that. It still has a few quirks with touch, though: it will automatically select multiple buttons if I use touch instead of just changing the focus from one button to another. Still, it's very much an improvement.
  • I haven't tried out the new feature of connecting an Android phone because my phone (Nexus 4) won't recognize that the GWG service is running on my tablet. Besides, the feature doesn't interest me as much as having touch controls on my tablet, which is why this new release disappoints me.
  • The advertised accelerometer support is really misleading. When I read that GWG2 featured support for Dirt 3 with the accelerometer, I got excited and made a gamepad using tablet accelerometer...only to find it still doesn't work. The only accelerometer support added was for phones. Sonic & All-Stars Racing Transformed can do it, why can't you?
  • Unfortunately, the XInput gamepad functionality hasn't worked in any game I tried either. It would certainly be a nice addition if it worked. TouchFox can do it, although it's severely limited in compatible games.
  • Although it may just be a one-off, I still have a huge slowdown issue with Rayman Origins.

Overall, I'm pretty disappointed to see that it's still the same engine that was in GWG1. Android controllers could be a great feature that makes GWG more popular, but don't forget why we used your product in the first place.

Hello thoe.prous, thank you for the feedback. As you know we are in beta so this feedback is really helpful as we test to release. Let me see if I can go over your points. Note that sometimes it gets so hectic we miss a post response, but rest assured we always read and consider them!

  • for the first one do you mean if you click a button then click another button it will highlight both? I dont have this issue, if i may ask what machine are you using to play games and gwg with?

  • we are still trying to smooth out the remote device connections, but when it works it works really well and is actually really enjoyable. It lets you connect to a bigger screen and best of all, you can play with your friends.

  • for accelerometer support on your tablet, it is a little bit buried at the moment because we are still trying to tweak it and havent finished all the features, but when your in your dirt3 VC go to the properties tab and on the right is a spot called "experimental" check the "enable senors". Now go to global gestures and on the top it says "touch" and "sensor", go to the sensor tab and there should be tilt controls.

  • for xinput, when your making your left and right sticks, as well as your buttons you have to scroll down near the bottom of the assigning list. We are currently considering the way the left stick works, and have ran into a problem with the right stick not working properly - other times it does. Whenever they both map as the same thing (incorrectly), please make a bug report with the logs so we can find the problem and fix this.

  • I will test rayman in the new build today!

Thank you very much for that feedback, we are striving to make the most intuitive and user unique experience we can.
