I've spent the evening playing SC2 and it plays decent with GWG. I'd like some feedback on what to put into the VC and how reliable the gestures are before submitting the controller, though. Here's a link to the XML that can be imported: https://skydrive.live.com/redir?resid=D63FA12AD7AC5DD8%213758

One finger does what you'd expect (left click, drag-select, etc). Unfortunately, taps are sometimes interpreted as double-clicks. Please confirm.

Two finger tap is a right click. The target is directly between the two fingers.

Drag two fingers across the screen to pan the map. Make sure and disable mouse scroll in the game settings ('Controls' section); otherwise, the invisible mouse cursor will end up at the edge of the screen and continue panning the map after your fingers are off the screen.

The three buttons on left are ctrl, alt, shift (not necessarily in that order). The best on-screen buttons for this game kind of depend on whether you play with the tablet on one arm (and only have one hand available), or you play with it on the table (and have two hands available). At first I used the ctrl-# buttons to assign groups, but ended up just right-clicking group buttons on the SC2 UI to assign groups, and ultimately used only the 'A' button and sometimes the '#' buttons on screen. I've played very little SC2 so I'd appreciate feedback from those of you who are familiar with essential controls.

GWG VC seems to work great for me for 10-30 minutes, but then a persistent ghost cursor sometimes appears in the exact center of the screen. I'd appreciate confirmation and feedback on the issue.

A zoom gesture is also included and does work, but I'm thinking of removing it because zoom seems irrelevant to the actual mechanics of SC2.

I understand that competitive (online) SC2 is a travesty without a keyboard, but there might be other people like me who have this game as part of their backlog and are interested in completing the campaign on their tablet for fun.

Also, I'm getting low FPS (15-20) on my Dell VP11 (atom). I thought I was getting ~30 FPS last week, but I don't remember for sure. I've tried a couple tweaks on top of putting all the settings to low and resolution to 720p, but it's still a bit too choppy, even for casual play.
