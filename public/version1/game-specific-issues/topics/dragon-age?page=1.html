OK so I have tested, and this is what I found: - I had no problem launching Dragon Age with a gamepad, whether directly, or via the start-up screen of Dragon Age - Most keyboard and mouse commands seem to work well through buttons and gestures - Two exceptions to that: 1. The joystick moves a very ghostly mouse pointer that flickers in and out of existence (mostly out), so that it cannot be use to really point at things. You have to use direct touch. 2. I can't for the life of me scroll up and down, which is critical in this game (to change from over the shoulder view to strategy/combat view). I tried with gestures (mapped to page up/page down and scroll up/scroll down) and buttons/scroll bar button (similarly mapped).

So for the moment, this game is almost there, but a no-go right now with just touch controls. I use a portable "air" mouse called the Ozupad 3-way to play it standing in the train.

Yannick
