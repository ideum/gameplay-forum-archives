Hi Kagesame1707,

I know what you're referring to with the Virtual Controller overlay appearing on the launcher and before you start a League of Legends match. There isn't a way to turn off the Gameplay overlay though and have it appear only in matches, but there are actually two simple things you can do to hide it until you need it!

The first, and easiest, would just be to use the Home button to hide the overlay. (The Home button is that little half-circle icon that appears on the side of the screen for any Virtual Controller.) The option to hide your HUD is that bottom button from the Home button's pop-out options.

The second option would just be to make your View One for your Virtual Controller have no controls on it, and then when you're in a match, you could use the Home button in game to switch to another View, the one with all the controls.

Hope that helps!

As for the D-Pad being configured more like an analog stick, that's a fair request that we've been receiving a lot of and are looking to addressing and discussing more about.

Is is possible that display scaling has not been disabled? I noticed the same thing with games on my surface pro and it was due to display scaling being enabled.

If you haven't already, try the following. Right click the exe's related to your game and click properties Click compatibility tab There is a tick box toward the bottom of the window along the lines of "disable display scaling..."

By default surface pros are shipped with display scaling set to 150%. This is due to the 10 inch screen with a 1080p display. Having scaling set to 100% makes everything that small text is almost unreadable.

If this doesn't help then perhaps there is an issue with gwg
