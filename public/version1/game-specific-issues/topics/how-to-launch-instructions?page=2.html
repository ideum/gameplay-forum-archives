Thank you very much for your activity on the forum. We are following your issue closely and working on ways to find a solution for you, and also improve the game launching workflow. Hopefully we can clear up any questions you have about game launch sequences. Looking at your earlier posts it appears you have the correct executable mapped to the Terraria Virtual Controller that you created: "program files\steam\steamapps\common\terraria\terraria.exe" and you have the Virtual Controller enables in the tool.

For Gameplay to interact with Steam games, it needs to be the entity that launches the Steam client. This means for Steam games, the user will never open the Steam client themselves. There are two times when Gameplay launches the Steam client.

1) When you first log in to your Windows 8 account, Gameplay automatically starts and it immediately launches the Steam client for you if you have a Steam client installed. All you should have to do when you first start your device is wait for Gameplay to launch Steam and then enter your username and password into the Steam client login dialog when prompted. If on first starting your device, you are not prompted to enter your Steam username and password, you probably have configured your Steam client to remember your username and password and log in is automatic. You can confirm successful Steam login by looking at your system tray and locating the Steam client icon.

2) The second situation where Gameplay is able to launch the Steam client is when you launch a game from the tool using the "Launch" button. This only works for games that automatically launch Steam if Steam is not already running. These types of games allow users to launch the game both from the Steam client and also by double clicking the executable in the install folder. Unfortunately, Terraria is not one of these games. Terraria is a game that can only be launched via the Steam client. As you may already know, if you navigate to the executable and try to run it you get an error: "Please launch the game from your steam client." This behavior prevents Terraria from being launched by double clicking the executable, and it also means it can't be launched directly from the Gameplay tool by clicking on the "Launch" button.

The steps needed to play Terraria with an overlay are: Log out or restart your device, wait for Gameplay to launch Steam (which it will do automatically), enter username and password if prompted, launch Terraria from the Steam client (not from the Gameplay tool). If these steps do not work, then there is an underlying problem with either your installation or your hardware and we would need more information about your device and possibly take a look at your debug logs.
