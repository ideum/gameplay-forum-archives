Hi all

I have just come across this software and website whilst searching for a gamepad suitable for an amputee to use. Currently my setup is a mouse and a generic USB gamepad with 8 very large buttons and a joystick (which I don't use) and a few other tiny buttons. I popped 4 of the buttons off and play Diablo 3 with a mouse (left hand) and the gamepad 4 buttons with my stump (right hand amputated at wrist). This allows me to hit the four large and separated buttons easily without accidentally hitting other buttons like I used to on a keyboard.

I play Diablo on two computers, my main PC attached to a projector in my rumpus room (awesome on a 100" screen) and my Asus 17" gaming laptop with attached 27" LCD screen on my desk in the lounge (and on the laptop when travelling or visiting friends etc). So I was thinking of buying a second of these controllers so I didn't have to move it between setups when I got to thinking about what else I could use which got me to this site and post.

Sorry for the long story but background info is often important.

So my questions is:

Firstly I have only read about this software being used on tablets, I actually have an Acer W700 with Diablo on it but don't really play, I just use it to update my auctions when out and about or even when lazing in bed so I don't have to go start up either of the PC's. So the first question is, can this software be used on a PC with a standard touchscreen plugged in? Obviously I'm thinking of having a separate screen maybe just laying on the desk (15" supermarket type touchscreen or something) that I can use as a virtual gamepad with custom made buttons sized and positioned to suit my stump made by your software.

Potentially I could just buy a large touchscreen monitor and lay it on an angle on my desk so as to play the game and touch the screen like you do with a tablet should this software work on a standard PC but not be able to be run on a separate monitor as from what I can see the software seems to run inside the game on all the demo video's I have seen so far. But obviously this eliminates the use of my projector setup which is not ideal. So I'm hoping this software can start the game but run outside of the game rather than over top of it so I can use my idea of a separate LCD touchscreen in place of a standard game controller?

So thanks for reading my long story and I look forward to any help or advice you have to offer.

Cheers, Bryden Zaloum (New Zealand)

Hey Inspector_NZ!

We're very happy and excited to be able to help you continue gaming with our software following your amputation. Considering you're able to customize the buttons and controls in Gameplay to best suit you, I feel like that could definitely be a nice advantage if you have Gameplay installed on something like a large touchscreen monitor, as you suggested.

To answer your questions, yes, Gameplay definitely works with a PC that has a touchscreen monitor. That's actually what a lot of us have for our setup here in our office. As for running outside the game itself though, as in having the Virtual Controller on one monitor with a game playing on a different monitor, that isn't something it currently is able to do, I am sorry to say. However, this is such an excellent suggestion, and I will be passing it along to our developers.

Thanks again for your interest and your great questions!! We really appreciate them and hope to better suit your circumstances.
